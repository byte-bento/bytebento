5 Curated Tech News Sources (Besides Us) That Are Actually Worth Your Time

Posted June 27, 2025

Let's face it: tech news is overwhelming. One minute it's Apple rumors, the next it's blockchain-for-goats startup funding.

We built ByteBento to cut through that — but we're not the only ones trying to make tech news more human. Here are five others we genuinely respect.

1. Techmeme

🧠 Type: Aggregator
📰 What it does well: Pulls top headlines from trusted tech sources in real time.
👍 Why it's useful: It's fast and reliable.
⚠️ Heads-up: Still a firehose. Best in small doses.

2. TLDR Newsletter

🧠 Type: Daily email
📬 What it does well: Summarizes top stories in tech and science.
👍 Why it's useful: Incredibly skimmable.
⚠️ Heads-up: No website. Email-only.

3. Benedict's Newsletter

🧠 Type: Weekly long-form
📊 What it does well: Smart analysis of tech trends.
👍 Why it's useful: You'll learn *why* stories matter.
⚠️ Heads-up: Not for quick updates.

4. Hacker News Front Page

🧠 Type: Community-curated
💬 What it does well: Surfaces dev-focused discussions.
👍 Why it's useful: Often shows deeper, lesser-known stories.
⚠️ Heads-up: Noisy, minimal design, and opinionated comments.

5. The Verge's “Hot Pod”

🧠 Type: Niche newsletter
🎙 What it does well: Covers podcasting and media tech.
👍 Why it's useful: Excellent if you're into audio.
⚠️ Heads-up: Niche focus.

🔖 Bonus: ByteBento (That's Us)

🍱 What we do differently: Byte-sized tech news in a clean, filterable format — without the noise.
